Section 2

Program Comparison

Compare real next-step programs or pathways you might actually attend or pursue.

For this section: compare real schools, programs, branches, organizations, unions, training centers, or other actual pathways. Try to get admissions, cost, and scholarship or aid information from their own pages, not just from a general overview site.
  • Use the exact page title.
  • Use the exact page-specific link.
  • Do not paste only a homepage.
  • For cost or funding, look for tuition, financial aid, scholarship, or admissions pages from the actual institution or organization.
Section 3

Highest Possible Level in This Field

Look beyond the entry-level version of this career and research how far this field can go.

For this section: if you are recording cost, tuition, scholarships, assistantships, grants, financial aid, or program funding, try to get that information from the actual school, program, or organization page connected to that higher level.
For some tracks, this may be less important than licenses, certifications, or advanced training.
